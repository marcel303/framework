Chaos

Four rings connected by joints. Each joint offering only one degree of freedom, a rotation around one single axis.

Systems like these, although seemingly simply, often create complex and chaotic movement.

The double pendulum is another variation on this principle. Simple systems, for which it is very hard to predict the exact movement even in the very near future.

Points along the rings can be sampled to produce complex moving (x, y, z) variables, which can in turn be used to drive the control variables used during sound synthesis.

The chaotic element seems to be a fundamental property of nature. In nature, systems often show complex behavior, which can be understood better through knowledge of chaos theory and how simple periodic systems (like a series of rings rotating around one axis), can become seemingly chaotic or 'unstable', as more variables are introduced into the system and combined with each other.

The rings can be made smaller and bigger, enlarging and shrinking the possibility space for the sampled (x, y, z) values. The cyclic expansion and contraction can be seen as a reference to the theorized big crunch which some physicists suggest could be the explanation for the big bang: a never ending series of expansions and contractions, under the influence of gravity.
